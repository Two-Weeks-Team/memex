What just happened

People don't document every memory — yet when the moment arrives, the relevant thought surfaces on its own.

Memex is a non-chatbot, LLM-free-at-runtime tool that reads your existing Claude Code and Codex session history and turns it into a navigable associative memory backed by Qdrant. You move through your own past by similarity, not keywords — and the relevant moment can rush toward you proactively, the way a forgotten fact returns mid-task.

— far layer —

Your history is the deep field

Every Claude Code & Codex transcript becomes a point in a vast vector space — a corpus you own, embedded and indexed in Qdrant.

— a current pulls —

The moment sends a signal

What you're debugging or deciding right now is matched by similarity against everything in the dark. No query typed — the situation itself is the prompt.

— near layer —

The scene swells into view

The closest past point rushes forward through the parallax layers and resolves into the full scene of that moment — you're the recipient, not the seeker.
