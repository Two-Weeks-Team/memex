What just happened

People don't document every memory — yet when the moment arrives, the relevant thought surfaces on its own.

Memex is a non-chatbot, LLM-free-at-runtime tool that reads your existing Claude Code and Codex session history and turns it into a navigable associative memory backed by Qdrant. You move through your own past by similarity, not keywords — and the relevant moment can decay out of its orbit toward you proactively, the way a forgotten fact returns mid-task.

— in its ring —

Your history quietly orbits

Every Claude Code & Codex session becomes a body circling a quiet core — a corpus you own, embedded and indexed in Qdrant. Nothing is hunted; everything waits.

— the orbit decays —

The moment tugs one inward

What you're debugging or deciding right now is matched by similarity. The closest past body loses altitude and spirals toward the front — no query typed.

— it blooms, then returns —

The scene swings to the front

At its nearest pass it blooms into the full scene of that moment, holds, then is flung back to its ring — you're the recipient, not the seeker.
