What just happened

People don't document every memory — yet when the moment arrives, the relevant thought surfaces on its own.

Memex is a non-chatbot, LLM-free-at-runtime tool that reads your existing Claude Code and Codex session history and turns it into a navigable associative memory backed by Qdrant. You move through your own past by similarity, not keywords — and the relevant moment can rise to you proactively.

Time Machine

Replay any past session as it happened — the whole scene, not a snippet.

Lens

Named-vector weighted search to look through your history from one angle at a time.

Topology

A distance-matrix → MST graph — the branching veins of how your sessions actually connect.

Mix & Discovery

Positive / negative recommendation to pull related work toward you and push noise away.

Proactive Recall

The piece that fronted the glass above: a relevant past moment surfaces because the current one evoked it.

Companion · Wrapped · Loop Breaker

An agent-memory layer over your corpus — recap what you've done, and catch yourself circling.
