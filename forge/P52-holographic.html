Why it rises on its own

01

The situation, not your search

Memex reads what you're working on now and matches it by similarity against your whole session history — no keyword, no query field.

02

A scene, not a snippet

What surfaces is a whole moment you can step back into: the file, the dead end, the line that finally worked — replayable, not just quoted.

03

What Memex actually is

  • A non-chatbot, LLM-free-at-runtime tool over your Claude Code / Codex session history (~/.claude/projects/**/*.jsonl + legacy transcripts).
  • It turns that history into a navigable spatial / associative memory, backed by Qdrant — you move by similarity, not keywords.
  • Surfaces: Time Machine replay, Lens (named-vector weighted search), Topology (MST graph), Mix / Discovery, proactive Recall, Dashboard, and a Companion / Wrapped / Loop Breaker layer.
  • It exists because Claude Code silently migrates and deletes old sessions — Memex keeps a corpus you own.
