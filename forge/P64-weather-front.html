What you just watched

You don't document every memory — yet when the moment is right, the relevant past surfaces on its own.

No one writes down every session. So Memex doesn't ask you to. It keeps a corpus of your Claude Code / Codex history — ~/.claude/projects/**/*.jsonl, legacy transcripts, and history.jsonl — and turns it into a navigable spatial, associative memory. As you work, the past you'd have lost arrives unbidden, the way a forgotten fix returns mid-task.

It is a non-chatbot, LLM-free-at-runtime tool. The map is built by Qdrant — you move through your own history by similarity, not keywords — so there is no model improvising answers, and no search box to interrogate.

Proactive Recall

The current task evokes related past sessions — they surface on their own, like the scene the front just rained down.

Lens

Named-vector weighted search across the corpus, for when you do want to steer the lookup yourself.

Topology

A distance-matrix to MST graph of how your sessions actually relate to one another.

Time Machine

Replay a past session in sequence — Claude Code silently migrates and deletes old ones; this keeps a copy you own.

Mix / Discovery

Positive / negative recommendation — pull toward what fits, push away from what doesn't.

Companion · Wrapped · Loop Breaker

The agent-memory layer: a running picture of your work, and a nudge when you're circling the same problem.
