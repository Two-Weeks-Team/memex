What just happened

People don't document every memory — yet when the moment arrives, the relevant thought surfaces on its own.

Memex is a non-chatbot, LLM-free-at-runtime tool that reads your existing Claude Code and Codex session history and turns it into a navigable associative memory backed by Qdrant. You move through your own past by similarity, not keywords — and the relevant moment can pull into focus proactively, the way a half-remembered fact returns mid-task.

— at the edge —

Your history sits in soft focus

Every Claude Code & Codex transcript becomes a point in a vector space — present but peripheral, a corpus you own, embedded and indexed in Qdrant.

— the moment reaches —

The situation calls it up

What you're debugging or deciding right now is matched by similarity against everything at the edge. No query typed — the moment itself is the prompt.

— it sharpens —

The scene pulls into focus

The closest past moment sharpens from blur into the full scene of when you worked it out — you're the recipient, not the seeker — then drifts back to the edge.
